PROJECTS

Computational Thinking through Play for Schools for the Blind in India
Designing and evaluating a culturally-sensitive play-based curriculum for CT and Numeracy that is accessible for the blind and easily implementable in a low-resource setting. ASSETS'19, ASSETS'19 Artifact Award (Runner Up), ICTD'20

Introducing Digital Games in Schools for the Blind in India
A mixed-method research study conducted to understand teacher's perception around introducing digital games to enahnce digital skills of blind children. CHI'21

Numeracy and Math games for blind children
Designing and evaluating play artifacts (Braille Factor Blocks, Braille Cards, Numberline Estimation games) to support early math education of young blind children in India. In submission at IxDA'21

Exergame for blind adults living in India
A qualitative research to gain an in-depth understanding of the barriers, challenges, and opportunities faced by blind people related to exercises and other fitness activities, and designing an exer-game that meets their needs in a low-resource setting environment. In submission.

Navigating Life with a Visual Disability
A qualitative research that explores life stories of blind adults (and their parents) while they navigate through numerous challenges and socio-cultural barriers to receive STEM education in India.

Tangible Collaborative Game for Enhancing Spatial Skills of the Blind
Blind children lack awareness and understanding of their surroundings. To enhance their spatial skills, designed and prototyped a multi-sensory collaborative tabletop game, using a participatory design approach with O\&M instructors of Singapore Association of Visually Impaired.

TouchPIN: Accessible Authentication System for Smartphone Users who are Blind
Blind smartphone users face various threats of observational attacks while entering passwords in a public place. Conducted a contextual research study, designed an accessible authentication system (TouchPIN) based on tactile cues and conducted a usability testing evaluating users' performance on TouchPIN in various use case scenarios.

(Project Kanimuni) Educational Games
Designed an educational board game based on concepts of Electricity and Magnetism, for children studying in Government schools of Guwahati, Assam.
