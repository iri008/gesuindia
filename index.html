Hello there, I'm Gesu :)

Human-Computer Interaction x Accessibility x Artificial Intelligence

I'm a second-year PhD researcher at the Computational Foundry, Swansea University. In my PhD, I’m exploring and designing the disability-first methodologies and resources for ethical collection of datasets with blind or low-vision people in low-income countries. I’ll soon be conducting a large-scale (object) dataset collection with blind/low-vision people in India, so would love to chat if you’ve worked or are currently working on something similar :)

Before joining my PhD, I spent three years at Microsoft Research India as a Research Fellow. Before that, I was an undergrad at Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Patna. I’ve also been fortunate to work at IIT Bombay, Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD), and IIT Guwahati for research internships during my undergraduate studies.
I’ve been active in the HCI research community since 2016, and have contributed to projects such as, (usable security) designing an accessible-authentication for low-end smartphone users who are blind/low-vision, (pedagogy) designing an accessible Computational Thinking curriculum for children studying in the low-resource schools for the blind in India, (fitness) designing a smartphone app using virtual exploration of real-world locations to promote fitness among blind/low-vision people, and several more. You can find more details in the Project section. :)
Lastly, I’m also suuuper passionate about art, creativity, cooking, singing, photography, and fashion. Also, I love to chat, so feel free to email/message/tweet at me with questions about my research, hobbies, and anything in between :)
