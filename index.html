Hi. I'm Gesu.

Human-Computer Interaction | Accessibility | STEM education

For past two years, I am placed at Microsoft Research India, where I am working as a Research Fellow with Dr. Manohar Swaminathan. Prior to MSR, I was finishing my undergrad at Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Patna. My research broadly lies at the intersection of HCI, Accessibility and STEM education. Derived from my research and personal experiences, I'm interested in understanding the dynamics of disabilities and how people relate to their disabilities over the course of their life --- in their culture, in their relationships, and as a part of their identity.

At MSR, my research focuses on designing and evaluating a culturally-sensitive play-based curriculum for Computational Thinking and Numeracy that is accessible for children with vision impairments and easily implementable in a low-resource setting. Other current and past research topics include designing and evaluating technologies for people with disabilities in order to improve their experiences with -- navigation and mobility; spatial skills; exercises and other fitness activities; accessible and secure authentication on smartphone in public places; and many more.
